Conservation

Grade 11-12 Advanced

Ethiopian Biodiversity

  • 6,000+ plant species (10% endemic)
  • 320 mammal species (including Ethiopian wolf)
  • 860 bird species (30% of Africa's total)
The Bale Mountains are home to more endemic species than any other area in Africa.

Protected Areas

  • Simien Mountains National Park (UNESCO)
  • Bale Mountains National Park
  • Awash National Park

Climate Science

Grade 11-12 Advanced

Climate Change Impacts

  • Temperature increase: +1.7°C since 1960
  • Rainfall variability: 15-20% decrease in some regions
  • Desertification: 135,000 hectares/year lost
Ethiopia's Green Legacy Initiative planted 25 billion trees since 2019.

Renewable Energy

  • Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam (6,450 MW)
  • Wind farms (Ashegoda, Adama)
  • Geothermal potential (Rift Valley)

Sustainability

Grade 11-12 Advanced

Sustainable Agriculture

  • Terracing techniques in highlands
  • Agroforestry systems
  • Organic coffee production
Ethiopian teff is a climate-resilient crop with high nutritional value.

Waste Management

  • Addis Ababa's Reppie Waste-to-Energy plant
  • Community composting programs
  • Plastic recycling initiatives

Daily Environmental Facts

Did You Know?

Ethiopia's Great Rift Valley is considered the "cradle of humankind" with some of the oldest hominid fossils ever discovered.

Climate Fact

The Ethiopian highlands are Africa's largest continuous mountain ecosystem, often called the "Water Tower of Africa".

Conservation

Ethiopia has successfully increased its forest cover from 3% to 15% in the last decade through massive reforestation efforts.

Innovation

Ethiopian scientists are developing drought-resistant crop varieties to combat climate change impacts on agriculture.
